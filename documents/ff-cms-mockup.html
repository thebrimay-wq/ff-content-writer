Can and should you get life insurance on a child?

Few financial topics generate more mixed feelings than life insurance on a child. It's not widely understood, and the sales pitch often moves faster than the math.

Most children don't need life insurance. A policy exists to replace income, and children don't have income to replace. The case for child life insurance — when it exists at all — is about insurability: locking in a small policy now guarantees a child who later develops a health condition can still be insured as an adult.

When a small policy makes sense

  • Family history of conditions that can affect future insurability
  • A burial/funeral buffer some parents want for peace of mind
  • Whole-life policies with modest cash value — rarely the best investment vehicle, but some families value the forced savings

When it almost certainly doesn't

If you don't already have 10–12x your income in term life on the earning parents, fund that first. A parent's death is the catastrophic financial event a child needs insured — not the reverse.
